Mantas

Description

Mantas is a dish of traditional Caucasian cuisine, which consists of dumplings made of thin puff pastry, stuffed with minced meat (most often beef or lamb) with the addition of onions and spices.

Mantas are usually steamed and served with sauce or sour milk. It is a popular dish in the countries of Central Asia, the Caucasus and other regions.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Minced meat (commonly beef or lamb)
  • Onions
  • Spices (such as black pepper, cumin, coriander, and paprika)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    1. Mix flour, water, and a pinch of salt to form a smooth dough.
    2. Knead the dough until it is elastic and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    1. Combine minced meat (commonly beef or lamb), finely chopped onions, salt, pepper, and other desired spices in a bowl.
    2. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to create a flavorful filling.
  3. Shape the Mantas:
    1. Roll out the dough thinly and cut it into small squares.
    2. Place a small amount of filling in the center of each square.
    3. Fold the dough over the filling to create a triangular shape, then pinch the edges together to seal.
  4. Cooking:
    1. Steam the mantas in a steamer for about 20-30 minutes until the dough is cooked and the filling is tender.
  5. Serving:
    1. Serve the mantas hot with sour cream, butter, or your preferred sauce.
